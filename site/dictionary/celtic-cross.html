Celtic Cross

Divination
REVIEWED HIGH SEED_DATA

The most widely used tarot spread, consisting of 10 cards in a cross-and-column layout.

Popularized by Arthur Edward Waite in 'The Pictorial Key to the Tarot' (1910). Ten positions: The Heart (central issue), The Crossing (challenge), Foundation (unconscious basis), Recent Past, Crown (best outcome), Near Future, Self-Perception, Environment, Hopes and Fears, and Outcome. The cross examines the situation's core; the column explores the querent's relationship to it.

Significance

The Celtic Cross generates the richest structural signals: trump density, suit prevalence, number sequences, and reversal patterns all become visible across its 10 positions.
