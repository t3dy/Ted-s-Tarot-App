Dictionary of Tarot

29 terms across 6 categories.

Card Structure

Court Cards

The 16 face cards: Page, Knight, Queen, and King of each suit.

Major Arcana

The 22 trump cards of the tarot deck, numbered 0-XXI.

Minor Arcana

The 56 suited cards divided into four suits of 14 cards each.

Pip Cards

The numbered cards (Ace through Ten) of each suit.

Trump

Another name for the Major Arcana cards, from the Italian 'trionfi' (triumphs).

Divination

Cartomancy

Fortune-telling or divination using playing cards or tarot cards.

Celtic Cross

The most widely used tarot spread, consisting of 10 cards in a cross-and-column layout.

Querent

The person for whom a tarot reading is being performed.

Reversal

A card dealt upside-down, traditionally indicating altered, blocked, or shadow meanings.

Significator

A card chosen to represent the querent or the question before the reading begins.

Spread

A specific layout pattern for dealing tarot cards, where each position has a defined meaning.

History

Court de Gebelin

Antoine Court de Gebelin (1725-1784), who first claimed tarot had Egyptian occult origins.

Eliphas Levi

Alphonse Louis Constant (1810-1875), the occultist who linked tarot to Kabbalah.

Etteilla

Jean-Baptiste Alliette (1738-1791), the first professional tarot cartomancer.

Sola Busca

The first complete 78-card tarot deck with fully illustrated (scenic) Minor Arcana, engraved c. 1491.

Visconti-Sforza

The oldest surviving tarot cards, painted in Milan c. 1440-1450 for the ruling Visconti and Sforza families.

Interpretive Method

Dialectical Reading

A reading method where reader and querent engage in mutual exploration rather than one-directional interpretation.

Fool's Journey

The concept of the Major Arcana as a narrative arc of spiritual development from The Fool (0) to The World (XXI).

Structural Reading

Reading the overall patterns of a spread (suit prevalence, trump density, number sequences) before interpreting individual cards.

Kabbalah

Decan

A 10-degree division of the zodiac, used to assign astrological correspondences to Minor Arcana pip cards.

Elemental Dignity

The system of elemental interactions between adjacent cards in a spread.

Hebrew Letter

One of 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet, each assigned to a Major Arcana card.

Path

One of the 22 connecting lines on the Tree of Life, each corresponding to a Major Arcana card.

Sephirah

One of the ten emanations on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, each corresponding to a number in the Minor Arcana.

Traditions

Book T

Mathers's secret Golden Dawn document establishing tarot-Kabbalah-astrology correspondences.

Golden Dawn

The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn (1888-1900), the magical society that systematized modern tarot correspondences.

Rider-Waite-Smith

The most widely used tarot deck, created in 1909 by Arthur Edward Waite and Pamela Colman Smith.

Tarot de Marseille

The historical French tarot pattern, the oldest continuously produced tarot design.

Thoth Tarot

Aleister Crowley's tarot deck, painted by Lady Frieda Harris (1938-1943).
